Lutos

Dubbed “The Cleansed Realm” by its citizens, Lutos rules magic with an iron fist. Centuries ago, the country constructed a prison to detain mages, and the officials of every city conduct yearly evaluations of their youth for signs of magic. The mage prison, Umbra, referred to by the mages as The Shadows, brands mages with a hot iron, fills their soulthreads in with ink to severe the bonds, and collars them with magic-suppressants.

Atoth

Atoth was given the nickname “The Unbound Realm” because they do not share the same sentiments as Lutos when it comes to magic. In Atoth, mages live freely as active participants in society. Mages often use their talents professionally as healers, blacksmith aides, irrigators, soldiers, and more. Through the contributions of magic, Atoth is slightly more advanced than Lutos and has more military power, which dissuades Lutos from engaging in hostility.

Fokos

Little is known about Fokos but much is speculated. Many believe it is the continent the elves originated from, but there is no evidence to confirm that. Supposedly, the elves fled Fokos, commonly called “The Lost Lands”, after a great fire or volcanic eruption that left the entire realm burning. Lutian and Atothian kings of old have sent their military fleets to investigate and potentially claim new lands, but none have ever returned.
